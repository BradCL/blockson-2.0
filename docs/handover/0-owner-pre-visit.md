# Step 0 — Get the owner ready (send this before the visit)

This is the one document you send **ahead of time**, not at the install. Forward it to
the owner about a week before you sit down together. Everything below the line is
written *to the owner*, in plain language — send it as-is.

Its whole job is to make the assumption [Step 1](1-account-setup.md) quietly leans on —
that the owner is in the room with an email they control, a phone for 2FA, and a card
if a new domain is needed — actually true on the day. The longest pole by far is the
one item you *can't* do for them: tracking down where their current domain lives. If it
sits inside a previous developer's account or a platform like Wix, Squarespace, or
GoDaddy, freeing it can take days and needs the owner's own logins and confirmation
clicks. Found a week early, that costs nothing; discovered at the install, it strands
the launch. That's the reason this goes out first.

# Setting up your new website — what to have ready

We'll spend about an hour together at your computer getting your new site live and
putting an **"Edit My Site"** button on your desktop. By the end, you'll publish a
change yourself. To make that hour go smoothly, please have these ready:

1. **An email you control** (and its password). This becomes your single login for your
   website, its hosting, and your web address — so the whole thing stays in your name. A
   business email is ideal.
2. **Your phone.** These accounts text or app you a security code when you sign in
   (two-factor login). That's what makes them truly, and only, yours.
3. **A payment card** — *only* if we register a brand-new web address (about
   $10–15 a year). The web address is the one and only thing in all of this that costs
   money; everything else is free.
4. **Where your current website's address lives — please find this out before we meet.**
   That means whoever built or hosts your current site, or the platform it's on (Wix,
   Squarespace, GoDaddy, and the like), plus any login you have for it. This is the one
   piece that can take **days** to sort out, because only you can approve moving it — so
   let's track it down ahead of time, not while the clock is running during our visit. If
   you're not sure where it lives, that's fine: just tell me before the day and we'll
   work it out together.
5. **The computer you'll edit from**, with internet.
6. **(Optional) Any new photos or wording you already want changed** — so your first real
   edits can be the ones you actually care about.

## What stays yours

None of this ties you to me, or to anyone:

- Every account is created in **your** name and **your** email.
- Your web address, your site's source, and your hosting are all yours.
- It's **free except the web address** — there are no monthly bills.
- You can hand the whole thing to **any** web developer later. You're never locked to
  one person.

That's the whole idea: a website that's genuinely yours, that you can edit yourself, and
that nobody can hold over you.
